We are living in one of the most volatile periods in human history. The geopolitics of the 20th century was about Oil and Land. The geopolitics of 2025 is about Data, Semiconductors, and Artificial Intelligence.

The world is shifting from a unipolar (US-led) order to a multipolar system. Let's analyze the current global situation through three main lenses.

The biggest conflict today is not happening on a battlefield, but inside silicon chips. Modern warfare and economy depend on Semiconductors (Chips).

Why Chips Matter?

  • They power everything: Missiles, iPhones, AI servers, and Cars.
  • TSMC (Taiwan) produces 90% of the world's advanced chips.
  • The US is trying to block China's access to advanced chips to slow down their AI and Military progress.

2. AI as the New Nuclear Weapon ü§ñ

Just as nuclear weapons defined the Cold War, Artificial Intelligence (AI) defines modern power. Countries are racing to achieve AGI (Artificial General Intelligence).

The country that dominates AI will dominate:

  • Cyber Warfare: Hacking enemy grids instantly.
  • Biotechnology: Creating new medicines (or viruses).
  • Economy: Automating entire industries.

3. The Rise of the Global South üåè

Countries like India, Brazil, Saudi Arabia, and Indonesia are no longer just picking sides between the US and China. They are asserting their own interests.

This is called "Multi-Alignment". They trade with China, buy weapons from France, and partner with the US for technology. This strategic neutrality is reshaping global alliances (like BRICS).

For smaller nations like Nepal, this geopolitical shift presents both risks and opportunities. Caught between two giants (India and China), our strategy must be one of balanced diplomacy.
